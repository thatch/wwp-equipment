    The panorama was taken with <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20010220201145/www.peleng-club.co.uk/content.htm" target="_blank">Peleng MC f 3.5/8 mm lens</a> (set at f 8, ~ 1.5 m) on <a href="http://www.eos-magazine.com/EOS%20digital%20cameras/EOS%20system/Digital%20cameras/EOS%2020D/EOS20Dkey.html" target="_blank">Canon EOS 20D camera</a>, (set at: 1/350, 1/1000, 1/125s (normal, underexposed, overexposed); ISO 200). <br><br>
    The panorama was stitched with 5 images (4 around plus zenith) using <a href="http://webuser.fh-furtwangen.de/~dersch/" target="_blank"> Professor’s Helmut Dersch Panorama Tools</a> (PTGui 4.1 graphical interface). <a href="http://autopano.kolor.com/" target="_blank"> Autopano</a> and <a href="http://enblend.sourceforge.net/" target="_blank"> Enblend</a>, as well as <a href="http://www.gimp.org/" target="_blank">Gimp&#39;s</a> ability to create Text Circle Logo were all of great help. <br><br>
    I used 8 bit source images. Each of the 5 source images were produced from 3 images (underexposed, normal and overexposed) by <a href="http://wiki.panotools.org/Enfuse" target="_blank">enfuse</a>. This was to produce much better looking images, thanks to Erik Krause :)<br><br>
    Equi-rectangular image was converted to QT with registered version 1.6.2 of <a href="http://www.pano2qtvr.com/index.php" target="_blank">Pano2QTVR</a> pro.
